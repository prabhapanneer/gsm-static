Fixed Deposits & Bonds

A simple explanation of what fixed deposits and bonds are, how they differ, where they may be useful, and how GSM helps clients choose the right place for the right purpose.

Not every rupee has the same job.

Some money is meant for long-term growth. Some money is meant for safety. Some money may be kept aside for regular income, short-term needs, or better stability in an overall financial plan. That is where fixed deposits and bonds can become useful.

What is a Fixed Deposit?

A fixed deposit, or FD, is a simple arrangement where money is placed with a bank or eligible financial institution for a fixed period at a predetermined rate of interest.

In simple terms, you keep aside a sum of money for a chosen period, and in return, you earn interest on it. Since the interest is usually known in advance, many people find it easier to understand and more predictable.

What is a Bond?

A bond is slightly different.

In simple terms, a bond is a structured way of lending money to an issuer such as the government, a company, or another eligible institution. In return, the issuer pays interest and repays the amount at maturity, based on the bond terms.

So while an FD is more like placing money in a fixed deposit arrangement, a bond is more like lending money through a formal investment instrument.

FD and Bond. What is the practical difference?

If a person wants to keep money in a more familiar and straightforward arrangement for a fixed period, that is closer to the idea of an FD.

If a person is comfortable lending money through a structured investment route for a defined period in return for interest, that is closer to the idea of a bond.

Both can have a place in financial planning, but they should be selected based on purpose, time period, and comfort level, not just because one option appears to offer a higher return.

Where they may be useful

Fixed deposits and bonds may be useful in situations such as regular income needs, short-term or medium-term parking of money, retirement-oriented cash flow planning, stability for a part of the portfolio, or specific goals where predictability matters more than aggressive growth.

Closing line

Fixed deposits and bonds are not meant to be exciting. Their role is often stability, predictability, and balance. That is exactly why they matter in the right place.
